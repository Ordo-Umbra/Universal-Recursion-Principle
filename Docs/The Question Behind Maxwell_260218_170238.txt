 2. The URP Field and Gauge Sectorization

URP starts from a single field φ(x,t) representing information density and structure. Its dynamics in the overdamped regime are:

∂_t φ = D ∇² φ + β |∇ φ|² + G V[φ],

with:

- Diffusion D ∇² φ: smoothing, causality.
- Nonlinear term β |∇ φ|²: **complexity pressure** that creates and sharpens distinctions.
- Coherence term G V[φ]: advection toward high-coherence regions; this is where gravity comes from.

In curved regions of the field, the nonlinear term does something subtle. It doesn’t just make gradients bigger; it **sectorizes**:

β |∇ φ|² ⟶ Σ_R β_R |D_R φ|²,

where each D_R is a **covariant derivative**

D_R = ∂_μ − i g_R A^R_μ

with emergent gauge fields A^R_μ and couplings g_R.

This “β-sectorization” is where the SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1) structure came from in earlier work:

- SU(3) from three-phase structure in high-curvature regions (color charge).
- SU(2) from two-phase chiral structure.
- U(1) from a **single phase degree of freedom**—the simplest possible gauge sector.

Electromagnetism lives in that last sector.

 3. U(1) as the Pure Phase Channel

 3.1 A Single Phase of Information

Take a local patch of the field and write its configuration as a complex amplitude:

ψ(x) = √ρ(x) e^{iθ(x)},

where:

- ρ is an information density (like probability or charge density).
- θ is a **phase**—the simplest possible kind of distinguishable structure.

If S-maximization wants to keep phase relationships coherent over distance, it must make **relative phase** physically meaningful but absolute phase irrelevant. That’s exactly what a U(1) gauge symmetry is:

- Gauge transformation: θ(x) ↦ θ(x) + α(x).
- To keep physics invariant, you introduce a field A_μ that shifts as:

  A_μ ↦ A_μ + ∂_μ α(x).

This is the standard gauge transformation of electromagnetism, but here it has a very concrete interpretation:

> You can add an arbitrary local “offset” to your information phase, as long as you adjust the bookkeeping field A_μ so that **mutual information between different points stays the same.**

The electromagnetic potential is literally **a correction field that tracks relative phase information** across the medium.

Among all possible gauge sectors, U(1) is special:

- It has no self-interaction term like the non-abelian SU(2), SU(3) fields.
- Its quanta (photons) are massless: there is no “coherence clustering” term to tie them down.

In S-language:

That makes U(1) the natural **“pure information channel”** of the field.

4. Maxwell’s Equations as S-Conserving Information Flow

Now we can see where the usual EM equations come from.

 4.1 The Action for the Phase Sector

In the U(1) sector, the URP Lagrangian picks up the familiar gauge kinetic term:

ℒ_EM = −(1/4) F_μν F^μν + J^μ A_μ

where F_μν = ∂_μ A_ν − ∂_ν A_μ, and J^μ is a current sourced by the motion of charged information.

Why this form?

Because among all possible quadratic terms you can build from A_μ, the −¼ F² one is:

- Gauge-invariant (respects the “only relative phase matters” rule).
- Lorentz-invariant (respects causal structure set by diffusion).
- The **minimal S-penalty** term that still allows nontrivial dynamics.

Anything more complicated would add unnecessary complexity without extra coherence.

4.2 Maxwell as Optimal Phase Transport

Varying this action with respect to A_μ gives

∂_μ F^μν = J^ν,

which unpack into:

- ∇·E = ρ  
- ∇×B − ∂_t E = J  
- ∇·B = 0  
- ∇×E + ∂_t B = 0

in the usual vector form.

Interpreted informationally:

- ∇·E = ρ: **source term**—places where information density is unbalanced (charge) give you divergence in the “update field”.
- ∇×B − ∂_t E = J: **current-coupled curl**—moving information (current) twists the field in such a way that phase correlations stay consistent in time.
- The two homogeneous equations express the **integrity of phase tracking**: no “magnetic charges” that would break closed loops, and the curl relationship that ensures path-independence of accumulated phase.

Maxwell’s equations, in URP language, are just:

> The unique set of local update rules for A_μ that maintain maximal mutual information between charged regions at minimal complexity cost, under the constraints of causality.

Once you adopt the S-view, standard EM phenomena take on a unified meaning.

 5.1 Charge: Fixed Asymmetry in Information Density

Electrostatically:

- Separating charges increases ΔC: more distinct configurations of the field.

Coulomb’s law, in URP terms, falls straight out of the same boundary-coherence reasoning used for gravity:

- The inverse-square falloff is the configuration that **maximizes boundary coherence per unit field energy** around a pointlike defect.

 5.2 Radiation: Exporting Excess Local Complexity

Accelerating charges or changing currents disrupt the local S-balance:

- To avoid decoherence, the system exports that complexity as **waves** that carry phase information outward at the maximum speed.

Those waves are just solutions of Maxwell’s equations in empty space:

∇² E − ∂_t² E = 0,  
∇² B − ∂_t² B = 0.

These are the **pure S-propagators**: patterns that maintain their internal correlations as they move, exactly like we expect from light.

 5.3 Polarization and Photons

Polarization is simply **which direction** your phase information is oscillating in transverse space—different ways of coding distinctions into the same channel.

In the URP symbol dictionary, the photon is a **toroidal vortex with zero net winding**—a loop of pure phase with no topological defect at its core. That is why:

Photons are literally **packets of update to the universe’s phase bookkeeping**, and electromagnetism is the infrastructure that makes them consistent.
