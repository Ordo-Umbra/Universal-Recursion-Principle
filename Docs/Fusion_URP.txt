## 1. Fusion as S‑Maximization

In standard physics, fusion happens when kinetic energy $$E_k$$ exceeds electrostatic repulsion $$E_c$$. In URP, fusion happens when the **integrated information** of the merged state exceeds the sum of the separated states:

$$
\Delta S = S_{\text{fused}} - (S_{\text{nucleus 1}} + S_{\text{nucleus 2}}) > 0

$$

**The breakthrough insight:** Instead of raising the temperature to force the reaction against low $$\kappa$$, we can **raise $$\kappa$$** by structuring the plasma to minimize local complexity in the reaction zone.

Deuterium‑Tritium (D–T) fusion releases 80% of its energy as fast neutrons, which degrade reactor walls and make them radioactive. 1.  **Beam‑Driven Coherence:** Instead of a hot soup, we maintain a cooler background plasma and inject **highly coherent beams** of protons.

3.  **Field‑Reversed Geometry:** We use a Field‑Reversed Configuration (FRC) to create a sharp, high‑coherence boundary layer. By driving this layer into a "super‑coherent" state (high $$\kappa$$), we maximize the probability of fusion events for the beam ions.

**The Result:** A reactor that burns clean fuel (boron is abundant and non‑radioactive), produces electricity directly from charged particles (no steam turbines), and is compact enough to fit on a truck.
